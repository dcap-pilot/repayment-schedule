How Banks Calculate Daily Interest (Reducing Balance Method)

Step 1: Convert Flat Rate to APR

  • Flat rate is the quoted rate (e.g., 3.5% p.a.)
  • APR/EIR is the effective rate used for daily calculation
  • Malaysia HP Formula converts flat → APR

Step 2: Daily Interest Calculation

  • Daily Rate = APR ÷ 365 (or 366)
  • Daily Interest = Principal × Daily Rate
  • Interest accrues every day on remaining balance

Step 3: Payment Allocation

  • Payment first covers accrued interest
  • Remaining amount reduces the principal
  • Tomorrow's interest is on the new lower balance

Understanding the Simulation

Flat Rate vs APR/EIR

  • Flat Rate: The advertised rate banks quote
  • APR/EIR: The actual effective rate used
  • APR is always higher than flat rate
  • Malaysia HP formula converts between them

Column Definitions

  • Opening Balance: Principal at start of day
  • Daily Interest: Interest charged for this day
  • Accrued Interest: Total unpaid interest accumulated
  • → To Interest/Principal: Payment allocation

Key Insights

  • Interest accrues daily on the outstanding principal
  • Payments first clear accrued interest, then reduce principal
  • Paying early reduces total interest (less days of accrual)
  • Larger payments = more goes to principal = less future interest
